High-energy astrophysics · AGN · reproducible discovery

X-ray astrophysics, accreting black holes, and reproducible discovery.

I study the X-ray emission of active galactic nuclei and radio galaxies — probing accretion, jets, and the iron Kα line as diagnostics of supermassive black holes. My PhD and postdoctoral work combined multi-mission X-ray spectroscopy (Chandra, XMM-Newton, NuSTAR) with carefully scripted, reproducible analysis.

Research themes

What I investigate

X-ray spectroscopy

Broad-band X-ray spectral analysis of nuclei and extended structures with XSPEC and CIAO.

Active galactic nuclei

The X-ray view of accreting supermassive black holes in radio-loud galaxies.

Supermassive black holes

Accretion physics and black-hole spin via relativistic reflection modelling.

Jets & radio galaxies

Jet–ISM interactions, hotspots, and lobes in nearby and powerful radio galaxies.

Fe Kα & reflection

Fluorescent iron-line emission and relativistic reflection as spin and geometry diagnostics.

Reproducible pipelines

Scripted, provenance-tracked workflows and scientific-software practices for repeatable results.

In preparation

Current research projects

Centaurus A X-ray source census In prep

A reproducible rebuild of a point-source census across multiple Chandra ACIS-I fields of the nearest radio galaxy — detection, photometry, and X-ray luminosity functions, scripted end-to-end. Scientific questions and software approach only; results remain unpublished.

  • CIAO
  • wavdetect
  • srcflux
  • Python

SMBH spin via relativistic reflection In prep

Joint NuSTAR + XMM-Newton spectral modelling with relativistic reflection (relxill) and MCMC to constrain black-hole spin — emphasising provenance, a documented software stack, and a fully reproducible fitting pipeline.

  • XSPEC
  • relxill
  • NuSTAR
  • MCMC
